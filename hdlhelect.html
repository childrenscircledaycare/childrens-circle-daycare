Programs Built Around Children: How Does Learning Happen?

Our programs are guided by Ontario’s How Does Learning Happen? framework and its four foundations for learning and development.

🤝

Belonging

Children feel connected to others and develop meaningful relationships within their family, community and environment.

❤️

Well-Being

Children develop a sense of self, health and confidence through nurturing relationships, active play and responsive care.

🔎

Engagement

Children become involved and focused as they explore their interests, solve problems and investigate the world around them.

🎨

Expression

Children communicate their thoughts, feelings and ideas through language, movement, creativity and play.

Emergent Curriculum & ELECT

Following Children’s Interests Every Day

At Children’s Circle Daycare, learning begins with children’s natural curiosity. Rather than following a fixed lesson plan, our educators observe children’s interests, questions, and discoveries, then create meaningful learning experiences that build on those moments.

Using the Early Learning for Every Child Today (ELECT) framework alongside Ontario’s How Does Learning Happen?, we support children’s social, emotional, cognitive, language, and physical development through play, exploration, and responsive relationships.

🧒

Child-Led Learning

Children’s interests inspire the experiences, projects, and activities we plan each day.

👀

Observation & Documentation

Educators observe, document, and extend children’s learning through meaningful interactions and thoughtful planning.

🌱

Learning Through Play

Hands-on exploration, creativity, and discovery help children develop confidence and important skills for life.

ELECT Developmental
Domains & Skills

At Children's Circle Daycare, our educators use the Early Learning for Every Child Today (ELECT) framework to observe children's growth, recognize emerging skills, and plan meaningful learning experiences. The domains and skills outlined in this document help guide our programming while recognizing that every child develops at their own pace.

We invite families to explore the developmental domains and skills that support children's learning and development throughout their early years.
